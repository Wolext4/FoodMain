Dietary Assessment

1. How many times in a week did you eat fast food?

2. How many times did you drink soda?

3. How many times did you eat two to three servings of vegetables?

4. How many times did you drink regular carbonated drinks (sodas), fruit juices, or glasses of sweet tea?

5. How many times did you eat beans, chicken without skin, or fish?

6. How many times did you eat regular chips or fried snacks (e.g., puff-puff, not low-fat)?

7. How many times did you eat ice cream and other sweets?

8. How much oil, butter, margarine, or meat fat do you use to season or cook or put on bread?

Healthy Eating Pattern:

Eating vegetables, fruits, beans, chicken without skin, or fish three times or more per week is **good** for your health.

Unhealthy Eating Pattern:

Eating regular chips, fried snacks, butter, margarine, meat fat, sodas, fruit juices, or fast food three times or more per week is **unhealthy**.
